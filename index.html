Phase 1 · The Science Foundation
Why Your Bones & Muscles Are Failing Together
In Progress ▼

The central insight: Bone and muscle don't decline independently — they share a biochemical crosstalk axis. Osteocalcin from bone drives glucose uptake in muscle; IGF-1 and myokines from muscle govern osteoblast activity. Targeting one without the other is why most programs fail after 55.

The mechanical threshold problem: Bone responds to strain above ~70% of its maximum — below that, no osteogenic signal is generated. Most senior exercise programs operate at 30–40% load. This phase closes the gap with a 3-tier progression calibrated to activate both fast-twitch fibers and the bone mechanostat simultaneously.

Phase 3 · The Nutrition Protocol
The Protein Timing Window That Rebuilds Bone (Most People Miss This)
Locked 🔒
🔒
Complete Phase 2 to unlock the Nutrition Protocol, your Leucine Reference Card, and 30-Day Workbook.

The post-exercise window: After resistance training, a 45-minute anabolic window opens during which leucine triggers mTORC1 and simultaneously elevates osteocalcin. After 55, anabolic resistance means the leucine threshold rises to 2.5–3g per meal — nearly double the younger adult requirement. Timing and threshold together determine whether you rebuild or continue to decline.
